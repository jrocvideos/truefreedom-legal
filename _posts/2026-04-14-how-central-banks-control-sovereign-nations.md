# The Invisible Leash: How Central Banks Control Sovereign Nations

Central banks aren't banks. They're control mechanisms dressed up in economic language.

Every sovereign nation that surrendered control of its money supply to a central bank essentially signed away its independence. We don't talk about this enough, and that silence is intentional.

## Debt As a Weapon

When a government needs money, it doesn't print it. It borrows from the central bank at interest. This creates perpetual debt that can never be repaid because the money to pay the interest was never created. It's a mathematical impossibility designed that way.

Nations become indentured to their central banks the same way a person becomes enslaved to a mortgage. The difference is the mortgage gets paid off eventually. National debt compounds forever. This isn't accident. It's architecture.

## Currency Control Is Sovereignty Control

The moment you can't control your own currency, you can't control your own destiny. The central bank decides interest rates, money supply, and inflation. They decide what your labor is worth. They decide who gets credit and who doesn't.

Elected governments—the ones we think represent us—become middle managers executing decisions made by appointed central bankers accountable to no one. This is governance without consent.

## The Policy Trap

Central banks don't need to ask permission. They use "monetary policy" to accomplish what military occupation used to require. Keep inflation high enough to erode real wages but low enough to avoid riots. Raise rates to crush dissent through unemployment. Lower them to reward obedience.

Governments comply because they're trapped. Resist the central bank and your currency collapses, your bonds become worthless, your economy tanks. Sovereignty exists only as long as the central bank allows it.

## The Global Synchronization

Every major central bank follows the same playbook because they're all connected—the Bank for International Settlements, regular coordination, shared ideology. When they moved in unison during COVID, flooding markets with currency while ordinary people lost livelihoods, we saw the real power structure.

Nations don't compete for your loyalty. They compete for access to capital that central banks control. That's the real hierarchy.

## What Independence Actually Looks Like

A truly sovereign nation prints its own money without interest debt. This seems radical only because we've normalized the unthinkable—that a nation must ask permission to create its own currency. Iceland tried it. Libya tried it. Both faced severe consequences, one internal, one external.

These examples weren't accidents either.

## The Reckoning

This system works until it doesn't. When confidence in currency dies, when debt becomes obviously unpayable, when people stop accepting the fiction that this is normal—that's when the real power transfers. Not to governments. To whoever holds actual assets.

Central banks have created the conditions for their own irrelevance through monetary debasement. They've also created desperation.

The question is not whether you see it. The question is what you do now that you do.
