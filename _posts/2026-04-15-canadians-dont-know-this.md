You know what's strange?

Most Canadians don't realize how fast things changed.

Gen X grew up in a different country. We had one phone in the kitchen. You watched the news at six o'clock and that was it. No algorithm deciding what you saw.

Back then freedom felt normal.

You could say what you thought, argue politics at the dinner table, and nobody thought the government should be moderating every conversation.

---

Now everything feels managed.

Information moves through filters. Platforms decide what spreads and what disappears.

And here's the part most Canadians don't know.

> Freedom rarely disappears overnight. History shows it fades quietly. Rules change a little. Language changes a little. People get used to things they once would have questioned.

Gen X watched the whole shift happen. From the analog world to the most controlled information environment in history.

---

So the real question isn't political. It's historical.

Are we paying attention to the direction things are moving?

**Because every generation eventually has to decide what freedom actually means.**

**And whether they're willing to protect it.**
